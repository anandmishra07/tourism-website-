THEKUA SWEETS!!


DESCRIPTION

Thekua, a traditional Jharkhandi sweet, is a delectable deep-fried pastry made from wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee. Its intricate shapes and rich, caramelized flavors make it a delightful treat, often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions in Jharkhand!

INGREDIENTS

1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
1/2 cup jaggery (gur), finely grated
2-3 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (elaichi)
1-2 tablespoons water (as needed)
1kg Oil for deep frying

DIRECTIONS

1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and finely grated jaggery.

2. Add ghee and cardamom powder to the mixture.

3. Mix all the ingredients together until they resemble breadcrumbs.

4. Gradually add a little water at a time and knead the mixture into a stiff dough. The dough should be firm enough to hold its shape.

5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat.

6. While the oil is heating, shape the dough into small, flat discs. You can use your hands or a cookie cutter for this.

7. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide the shaped Thekuas into the oil.

8. Fry them on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp on both sides. This should take about 5-6 minutes.

9. Remove the fried Thekuas using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

10. Allow them to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.
