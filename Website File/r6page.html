SANAI
KI
SABZI!!


DESCRIPTION

A traditional Jharkhandi dish, Sanai ki Sabzi features tender drumstick leaves cooked with aromatic spices. This flavorful and nutritious green delight is a staple in Jharkhandi cuisine, offering a taste of the region's authentic flavors.

INGREDIENTS

250 grams Sanai (also known as drumstick leaves)
2 tablespoons cooking oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, grated
2-3 green chilies, slit
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
11 teaspoon coriander powder
Salt to taste
1 cup water
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

DIRECTIONS

1. Start by cleaning the Sanai (drumstick leaves). Pluck the leaves from the stems and wash them thoroughly. Drain and set aside.

2. Heat oil in a pan or kadai over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter and release their aroma.

3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent and slightly brown.

4. Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and sauté for another 2 minutes to allow the spices to cook.

6. Now, add the cleaned Sanai (drumstick leaves) to the pan. Stir and cook for about 5 minutes until the leaves start to wilt.

7. Pour in 1 cup of water. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat. This will allow the Sanai to cook and become tender.

8. Check the seasoning and adjust salt and spice levels if needed.

9. Once the Sanai is cooked and the sabzi has a semi-dry consistency, remove it from heat.

10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
