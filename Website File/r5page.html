DHUSKA & SABJI!!


DESCRIPTION

Dhuska Sabzi is a popular dish in Jharkhand made by pairing crispy and fluffy rice and lentil pancakes (Dhuska) with a spicy and flavorful potato curry. It's a delightful combination of textures and tastes, representing the rich culinary heritage of the region!

INGREDIENTS

For Dhuska

1 cup rice 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
1/4 cup moong dal (split green gram)
1/4 cup masoor dal (split red lentils)
1 small onion, finely chopped
2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
1-inch piece of ginger, grated
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
Salt to taste
Oil for deep frying

For Sabzi

2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
1 cup cauliflower florets
1/2 cup green peas
1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
1/2 cup chopped onions
2-3 green chilies, slit
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
1 teaspoon garam masala powder
2 tablespoons oil
Salt to taste
Chopped coriander leaves for garnish

DIRECTIONS

For Dhuska
1. Wash and soak all the dals and rice together for about 4-5 hours.

2. After soaking, drain the water and grind the dals and rice into a smooth batter. Add a little water if needed to make a thick and smooth batter.

3. Add chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, asafoetida, cumin seeds, and salt to the batter. Mix well.

4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.

5. Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and deep fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.

6. Remove the dhuskas from the oil and place them on paper towels to remove excess oil.

For Sabzi
1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.

2. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Saute until the onions turn translucent.

3. Add diced potatoes, cauliflower florets, and green peas. Stir-fry for a few minutes.

4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.

5. Cover the pan and cook on low heat until the vegetables are tender, stirring occasionally.

6. Once the vegetables are cooked, add chopped tomatoes and garam masala powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil separates.

7. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
