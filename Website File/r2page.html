LITTI CHOKHA!!


DESCRIPTION

A rustic delight from Bihar and Jharkhand. Roasted wheat flour balls stuffed with spiced gram flour, served with smoky, spiced vegetable mash. An authentic regional favorite!

INGREDIENTS

For Litti

Whole Wheat Flour (Atta) - 2 cups
Gram Flour (Besan) - 1/2 cup
Ghee / Refined Oil - 2 tablespoons
Salt - 1 teaspoon
Baking Soda - 1/2 teaspoon
Water - As needed for kneading

For Filling (Sattu)

Sattu (Roasted Gram Flour) - 1 cup
Finely Chopped Onions - 1/2 cup
Green Chilies (Finely Chopped) - 2-3
Ginger (Grated) - 1 teaspoon
Garlic (Minced) - 1 teaspoon
Mustard Oil - 2 tablespoons
Lemon Juice - 1 tablespoon
Ajwain (Carom Seeds) - 1/2 teaspoon
Kalonji (Nigella Seeds) - 1/2 teaspoon
Salt - 1 teaspoon
Water - As needed

For Chokha

Roasted Eggplant (Baingan) - 2
Roasted Tomatoes - 4
Roasted Green Chilies - 2-3
Chopped Onions - 1/2 cup
Chopped Coriander Leaves - 2-3 tablespoons
Mustard Oil - 2 tablespoons
Garlic (Minced) - 1 teaspoon
Ginger (Grated) - 1 teaspoon
Salt - 1 teaspoon
Red Chili Powder - 1/2 teaspoon
Lemon Juice - 1-2 tablespoons

DIRECTIONS

For Litti
1.In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour (atta) with 1/2 cup of gram flour (besan).

2.Add 2 tablespoons of ghee (clarified butter), 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda to the flour mixture.

3. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a firm dough. Cover it and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

4. After resting, divide the dough into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into balls.

5. Preheat your oven or tandoor to a medium-high temperature.

6. Flatten each dough ball and place a portion of the sattu filling (described below) in the center.

7. Carefully seal the edges, ensuring the filling is enclosed within the dough.

8. Arrange the stuffed littis on a baking tray and bake until they turn golden brown and crisp.

For Filling (Sattu)
1. In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of sattu (roasted gram flour) with 1/2 cup of finely chopped onions, 2-3 finely chopped green chilies, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, 2 tablespoons of mustard oil, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1/2 teaspoon of ajwain (carom seeds), 1/2 teaspoon of kalonji (nigella seeds), 1 teaspoon of salt, and water as needed.

2. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly to form a slightly moist stuffing.

For Chokha
1. Roast 2 eggplants (baingan) and 4 tomatoes until their skins are charred and the flesh is soft.

2. Roast 2-3 green chilies as well.

3. Peel the roasted eggplants and tomatoes, and chop them finely.

4. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped eggplants, tomatoes, and green chilies with 1/2 cup of chopped onions, 2-3 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves, 2 tablespoons of mustard oil, 1 teaspoon of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, 1 teaspoon of salt, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, and 1-2 tablespoons of lemon juice.

5. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly to prepare the chokha.
